Other tourist spots to explore in Trivandrum

Palaces

Padmanabhapuram Palace



>

Padmanabhapuram Palace, 64 km from Thiruvananthapuram, showcases exquisite rosewood ceilings, murals, antique furniture, Durbar Hall, and royal bedroom, attracting art lovers daily except Mondays, from 9 AM to 5 PM.

The Kanakakunnu Palace



Kanakakunnu Palace near Napier Museum reflects royal craftsmanship, hosts cultural events, features chandeliers and antique furniture, includes auditoriums, and annually presents the Nishagandhi Dance Festival, attracting tourists to Trivandrum.

Parks and Zoo

Thiruvananthapuram Zoo


Established in 1857 beside Napier Museum, Thiruvananthapuram Zoo is a major conservation center, housing diverse animals, birds, reptiles, botanical gardens, orchids, and a snake farm with rich heritage and history.

Timings: Opened daily from 9 AM to 5:15 PM

Neyyar Dam and Wildlife Sanctuary


Neyyar Dam, built in 1958, near Trivandrum offers scenic picnic spots, medicinal herbs, and a wildlife sanctuary with safaris, trekking, elephant rides, and animal parks, making it a perfect one-day nature destination.

Veli Tourist Village


Veli Tourist Village, 8 km from Trivandrum, offers scenic lake-sea views, a floating bridge, restaurant, boating, adventure activities, and proximity to Kovalam, remaining open daily from 8 AM to 6 PM.

Hill Stations

Ponmudi

Ponmudi is a scenic hill station near Trivandrum, known for misty valleys, winding roads, lush greenery, cool climate, and breathtaking views of the Western Ghats
