Places to Visit in Trivandrum

Explore Trivandrum! - coastal city blending heritage, serene beaches, vibrant culture, ancient temples, lush landscapes, and captivating tourist attractions.

Beaches

Shangumugham Beach

Shangumugham Beach is a popular, serene coastal spot renowned for its stunning sunset views, white sands, and the massive Jalakanyaka sculpture.

Varkala Beach

Varkala Beach is renowned for its 15-million-year-old red cliffs along the Arabian Sea and offers a mix of spiritual significance, adventure, and relaxation with activities like surfing, yoga, and visiting nearby cafes and the 2,000-year-old Janardhana Swamy Temple.

Kovalam Beach

Kovalam is an internationally renowned, crescent-shaped beach town in Kerala, known for its Vizhinjam Lighthouse and three adjacent beaches offering a mix of rocky, white, and black sand shores.

Azhimala Beach

Azhimala Beach is a serene coastal spot near Trivandrum, famous for its rocky shores, golden sands, sunsets, and the towering Azhimala Shiva statue.

Thumba Beach

Thumba Beach is a peaceful stretch near Trivandrum, known for golden sands, gentle waves, fishing villages, and its proximity to India’s space research center.

Ashoka Beach

Ashoka Beach is a quiet coastal retreat near Trivandrum, offering calm waters, soft sands, palm-lined shores, and a peaceful escape from city crowds.

Places of Worship

Sri Padmanabhaswamy Temple is an ancient Hindu temple in Thiruvananthapuram, renowned for its Dravidian architecture, spiritual significance, and historical legacy dedicated to Lord Vishnu.

Attukal Bhagavathi Temple in Thiruvananthapuram is renowned for the Attukal Pongala, the world’s largest women-led religious gathering, celebrating devotion, faith, and cultural unity.

Aazhimala Siva Temple is a scenic coastal temple near Thiruvananthapuram, famous for its massive Shiva statue overlooking the Arabian Sea and its calm spiritual atmosphere.

Beemapally Masjid, located in Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala, is a sprawling mosque complex renowned for its traditional ornate architecture and spiritual significance.

St. Mary's Malankara Syrian Catholic Major Archieparchial Cathedral in Pattom, Thiruvananthapuram, is a stunning stone-built church over 70 years old, blending traditional architecture with modern touches.

Pazhavangadi Ganapathy Temple is a prominent temple dedicated to Lord Ganesha, located in the bustling city center of Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala. The temple is known for its unique idol of Lord Ganesha in the seated posture with his right knee folded.

Musuems

Kuthiramalika Palace Museum

Built by Swathi Thirunal Rama Varma, this 18th-century palace features 122 carved teak horses and intricate wooden ceilings. A masterpiece of Kerala architecture using rosewood and granite, it offers a rare glimpse into royal life of Travancore. Nearby attractions include the Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple and the iconic Methan Mani clock tower.

Napier Museum

Napier museum features historic coins, musical instruments, and intricate bronze sculptures of Hindu Gods. Its vast collection spans ancient dynasties, from the Cholas to the Romans. Nearby attractions include the Sree Chitra and Raja Ravi Varma galleries, Trivandrum Zoo, and Kanakakunnu Palace.

Sree Chitra Art Gallery

Located near Napier Museum, this gallery features 1,100 masterpieces, including Mughal, Rajput, and Oriental art. It showcases unique Tibetan Thangkas, Balinese paintings, and historic Indian murals. The adjacent Raja Ravi Varma Gallery displays world-famous works. It is conveniently located within the Napier Museum grounds near the Trivandrum Zoo.

Kerala Science and Technology Museum

This interactive hub features hands-on exhibits like the "Infinite Well" and "Nail Chair," alongside life-sized scientific artifacts. The complex includes the Priyadarshini Planetarium, a 3D theater, and a space gallery with spacecraft replicas, making science engaging for visitors of all ages.

Natural History Museum

The museum offers an impressive display of the animal kingdom, featuring from tiny ants to majestic whales. As you walk through you get transported into the natural habitats of these creatures, making it an educational and engaging experience. You’ll discover fascinating facts about wildlife and conservation, helping you appreciate the delicate balance of our ecosystems and the importance of protecting them.

Kerala Legislative Museum

The G. Karthikeyan Legislature Museum, located behind the Kerala Legislative Assembly in Thiruvananthapuram, is a two-story facility documenting the state's political history. It features three galleries, interactive kiosks, photographs of chief ministers, and a dedicated Mahatma Gandhi gallery, highlighting the evolution of Kerala's democratic, legislative assembly.

Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre

Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre (VSSC), Thiruvananthapuram, is the lead centre of ISRO responsible for the design and development of launch vehicle technology. The Centre pursues active research and development and undertakes crucial responsibilities of design, manufacturing, analysis, development and testing related to the realisation of subsystems for the different missions.

Sunil's Wax Museum

Pearl Art Gallery in Thiruvananthapuram specializes in handcrafted, premium shell pearl, and gemstone stainless steel jewelry. Offering a blend of modern elegance and traditional design, this boutique showcases unique, artisanal pieces.

More Places to Explore

Palaces

Padmanabhapuram Palace



>

Rejoicing in all its glory, the Padmanabhapuram Palace is located 64 km from Thiruvananthapuram in Thuckalay. The rosewood carved ceilings and a fine collection of paintings makes it one of the best specimens of Kerala.

Pictures of Lord Krishna, brass lanterns, mural paintings and a four poster medicinal bed add grace to the King’s bedroom. Details like the earthen urns, swimming bath, dance hall, fish carvings on ceilings, Belgian mirrors, 17th and 18th century frescoes and Saraswati Temple sum up to a pleasant structure.

The palace remains open each day from 9.00 AM to 5.00 PM. It is to be noted that visits on Mondays are not allowed.

The Kanakakunnu Palace



Neighbor to the famous Napier Museum stands Kanakannu Palace, an embodiment of excellent craftsmanship and architecture. This palace is the proud possession of the state of Kerala. Built by Sree Moolan Thirunal for holding royal ceremonies and banquets retains its image as a place of entertainment for the royal family. At present too, the palace plays host to many cultural programs. It has been named as the heritage monument by the Indian National Trust for Art and Cultural Heritage (INTACH).

Swathi Thirunal, who was a popular ruler of his time, constructed tennis courts in the premises of the palace.

Kanakakunnu Palace is the cultural hub of the city. The palace complex now owns two auditoriums, where a large number of cultural events take place. If you plan a visit, make sure you catch up the All India Dance Festival, popularly known as Nishgandhi festival hosted annually here.

Parks and Zoo

Thiruvananthapuram Zoo


Thiruvananthapuram Zoo, one of the oldest terrarium in the country which was established as a supplemental to the Napier Museum in the year 1857 by the former Maharaja of Travancore, which was originally opened for recreational purposes, the zoo becomes a conservation center of wildlife over a period of time.

The zoo has a wide array of 35 species of animals, 28 species of birds and 17 kinds of reptiles. The zoo also houses botanical gardens with more than 200 species of trees, thousands of beautiful plants, orchids and snake farm

Timings: Opened daily from 9 AM to 5:15 PM

Neyyar Dam and Wildlife Sanctuary


There are indeed number of things to do in Trivandrum, including paying a visit to picturesque Neyyar Dam and its neighbouring wildlife sanctuary. Built in 1958, the dam is an ideal family picnic spot which is set against the mesmerizing landscape. It is built where rivers like Neyyar, Kallar and Mullayar confluence. The area is famed for obtaining several medicinal herbs as well.

In the vicinity of this place, a wildlife sanctuary is well laid out. There are deer safari parks, crocodile and lion farms along with watchtower in the region. A guided trek, safari and elephant ride is a must here. Take out at least a day in order to get the best experience of the wild life sanctuary.

Veli Tourist Village


The planned location of the Veli Tourist Village proves to be a successful picnic spot. It is situated 8 kms from the city, where Veli Lake and Arabian Sea confluences. The charming landscape allures one to explore this much sort after destination.

Another attraction of this hamlet is its floating bridge, which is built recently. The village also houses a floating restaurant that offers sumptuous array of food products which indeed delights the taste buds.

Boat rides, rock climbing and other adventure activities offered here makes this one of the most preferred tourist places in Trivandrum. In close proximity of Kovalam, the village remains open between 8.00 AM and 6.00 PM every day.

Hill Stations

Ponmudi

Ponmudi is a scenic hill station near Trivandrum, known for misty valleys, winding roads, lush greenery, cool climate, and breathtaking views of the Western Ghats

Bonacaud

Bonacaud Hills is a tranquil hill retreat near Trivandrum, known for dense forests, cool climate, trekking trails, waterfalls, and rich biodiversity in the Western Ghats.
