Places to Visit in Trivandrum

Explore Trivandrum! - coastal city blending heritage, serene beaches, vibrant culture, ancient temples, lush landscapes, and captivating tourist attractions.

Beaches

Shangumugham Beach

Shangumugham Beach is a popular, serene coastal spot renowned for its stunning sunset views, white sands, and the massive Jalakanyaka sculpture.

Varkala Beach

Varkala Beach is a unique cliffside beach in Kerala, famed for dramatic views, spiritual vibes, natural springs, golden sands, sunsets, and a vibrant promenade overlooking the Arabian Sea.

Kovalam Beach

Kovalam is an internationally renowned, crescent-shaped beach town in Kerala, known for its Vizhinjam Lighthouse and three adjacent beaches offering a mix of rocky, white, and black sand shores.

Azhimala Beach

Azhimala Beach is a serene coastal spot near Trivandrum, famous for its rocky shores, golden sands, sunsets, and the towering Azhimala Shiva statue.

Thumba Beach

Thumba Beach is a peaceful stretch near Trivandrum, known for golden sands, gentle waves, fishing villages, and its proximity to Indiaâ€™s space research center.

Ashoka Beach

Ashoka Beach is a quiet coastal retreat near Trivandrum, offering calm waters, soft sands, palm-lined shores, and a peaceful escape from city crowds.


Places of Worship

Sri Padmanabhaswamy Temple

Sri Padmanabhaswamy Temple is an ancient Hindu temple in Thiruvananthapuram, renowned for its Dravidian architecture, spiritual significance, and historical legacy dedicated to Lord Vishnu.

Attukal Bhagavathi Temple

Attukal Bhagavathi Temple in Thiruvananthapuram is renowned for the Attukal Pongala, the worldâ€™s largest women-led religious gathering, celebrating devotion, faith, and cultural unity.

Aazhimala Siva Temple

Aazhimala Siva Temple is a scenic coastal temple near Thiruvananthapuram, famous for its massive Shiva statue overlooking the Arabian Sea and its calm spiritual atmosphere.

Beemapally Masjid

Beemapally Masjid, located in Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala, is a sprawling mosque complex renowned for its traditional ornate architecture and spiritual significance.

St. Mary's Malankara Syrian Catholic Major Archieparchial Cathedral

St. Mary's Malankara Syrian Catholic Major Archieparchial Cathedral in Pattom, Thiruvananthapuram, is a stunning stone-built church over 70 years old, blending traditional architecture with modern touches.

Pazhavangadi Ganapathy Temple

Pazhavangadi Ganapathy Temple is a prominent temple dedicated to Lord Ganesha, located in the bustling city center of Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala. The temple is known for its unique idol of Lord Ganesha in the seated posture with his right knee folded.


Museums

Kuthiramalika Palace Museum showcases exquisite wooden architecture, intricate carvings, royal artifacts, and musical instruments, reflecting Travancore heritage and the artistic legacy of Maharaja Swathi Thirunal.

Napier Museum in Trivandrum features Indo-Saracenic architecture, rare archaeological artifacts, bronze idols, ancient ornaments, and art collections, set amidst lush gardens and cultural heritage.

More Places to Explore

Palaces

Padmanabhapuram Palace

Padmanabhapuram Palace, 64 km from Thiruvananthapuram, showcases exquisite rosewood ceilings, murals, antique furniture, Durbar Hall, and royal bedroom, attracting art lovers daily except Mondays, from 9 AM to 5 PM.

The Kanakakunnu Palace

Kanakakunnu Palace near Napier Museum reflects royal craftsmanship, hosts cultural events, features chandeliers and antique furniture, includes auditoriums, and annually presents the Nishagandhi Dance Festival, attracting tourists to Trivandrum.

Parks and Zoo

Thiruvananthapuram Zoo

Established in 1857 beside Napier Museum, Thiruvananthapuram Zoo is a major conservation center, housing diverse animals, birds, reptiles, botanical gardens, orchids, and a snake farm with rich heritage and history.

Neyyar Dam and Wildlife Sanctuary

Neyyar Dam, built in 1958, near Trivandrum offers scenic picnic spots, medicinal herbs, and a wildlife sanctuary with safaris, trekking, elephant rides, and animal parks, making it a perfect one-day nature destination.

Veli Tourist Village

Veli Tourist Village, 8 km from Trivandrum, offers scenic lake-sea views, a floating bridge, restaurant, boating, adventure activities, and proximity to Kovalam, remaining open daily from 8 AM to 6 PM.

Hill Stations

Ponmudi

Ponmudi is a scenic hill station near Trivandrum, known for misty valleys, winding roads, lush greenery, cool climate, and breathtaking views of the Western Ghats

Bonacaud

Bonacaud Hills is a tranquil hill retreat near Trivandrum, known for dense forests, cool climate, trekking trails, waterfalls, and rich biodiversity in the Western Ghats.

Agasthyakoodam

Agasthyakoodam, Keralaâ€™s second-highest peak, lies in the Agasthyamalai Biosphere Reserve, offering challenging treks, rich biodiversity, and spiritual significance, with restricted access and permits required from January to March.

Meenmutty waterfalls

The Kallar Meenmutty Waterfalls is a prominent eco-tourism destination located in the Thiruvananthapuram district, approximately 45 km from the city center on the way to the Ponmudi hill station. It is a three-tiered waterfall accessible via a scenic trek through dense tropical forests.
