Places to Visit in Trivandrum

Explore Trivandrum! - coastal city blending heritage, serene beaches, vibrant culture, ancient temples, lush landscapes, and captivating tourist attractions.

Kuthiramalika Palace Museum or Puthenmalika Palace Museum built by Swathi Thirunal Rama Varma. Built in 18th century the palace is located a few yards away from the eastern entrance of Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple. The palace got its name from the figures of 122 horses carved in teak wood in a unique pattern that support the southern roof. All the rooms have different types of ceilings, carved in wood, small beautiful floral decorations are made. The roof of the palace on the ground floor is supported by granite pillars, which are adorned with intricate designs. Visit to the palace will give a glimpse of how the kings of Travancore had lived, though only portion of this palace is open for the public.Kuthiramalika Palace is a great example of the Kerala school of architecture and is made from teakwood, rosewood, marble, and granite. The Kuthiramalika museum houses idols and sculptures made from white marble, Kathakali figures, Belgian mirrors and paintings. The flourishing spice trade between Kerala and the world made it possible to obtain those items.

The Napier Museum has, on display, a vast variety of historically significant items. A large number of coins used during the rule of different dynasties can be seen here including coins from the prosperous kingdom of Vijayanagara, from the time of the Pandyas, the Cholas, Sivaganga coins as well Roman, Persian, Dutch, Chinese coins and many more. There is also a wide variety of musical instruments. The different ivory, wooden and bronze carvings and sculptures show Hindu Gods in a majestic artistry.Nearby attractions to the Napier Museum include the Sree Chitra Art Gallery, Raja Ravi Varma Art Gallery, the Trivandrum Zoo, and the Kanakakunnu Palace.

Sree Chitra Art Gallery, located in the northern grounds of the Napier Museum, features a unique collection of traditional and contemporary paintings. There are approximately 1100 paintings at the gallery including many timeless masterpieces.Apart from the Travancore paintings, the diverse collection also includes paintings from the Mughal, Rajput, Bengal, Rajasthani, and Tanjore schools of art. It also has an oriental collection of Chinese, Japanese, and Balinese paintings, Tibetan Thangka, and Indian mural paintings from pre-historic times. The gallery also houses various miniatures including 400-year-old Tanjore miniature paintings as well as manuscripts of archival importance. The Raja Ravi Varma Art Gallery is a separate gallery within Napier Museum that displays the major works of Raja Ravi Varma, his brother C.
